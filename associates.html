Head of Chambers

Advocate, Fair & Square Legal Associates

Perseverance, industriousness, and an unwavering pursuit of excellence epitomize the leadership at Fair & Square Legal Associates. Practicing at the District & Session Judge Court, Dhaka, our head of chambers brings a commitment to legal mastery and professional distinction.

Our firm serves a diverse clientele with expertise in Criminal, Civil, Family, Land, and Arthorin Ain law, providing trusted legal solutions locally and beyond.

Barrister Arshan Ali Saif

Managing Partner

Barrister Arshan Ali Saif is a skilled Barrister-at-Law with over six years of professional experience in litigation, legal advisory, and consultancy. Trained in the United Kingdom and practising in Bangladesh, he combines strong common law foundations with practical courtroom expertise.

He obtained his LLB (Hons) from the University of South Wales and completed his PGDL at Northumbria University before being called to the Bar of England and Wales by the Honourable Society of Lincoln’s Inn. He is also an Advocate of the District and Sessions Judge’s Court, Dhaka.

Barrister Saif specializes in Criminal Defence Litigation, Land and Property-related Litigations, and advises on Immigration Law and International Migration.

Barrister Kazi Afzal

Associate

Kazi Afzal is a dedicated legal practitioner with a strong commitment to excellence, having completed the Barrister Training Course and Master of Laws in Legal Practice at BPP University, London, in 2023. He is an ex-pupil of a corporate chamber in Dhaka, where he engaged in legal research, drafting, and vetting.

Advocate Ahmed Kibria

Associate

Ahmed Kibria, a distinguished Advocate, embodies a commitment to justice and excellence in legal practice. With over 11 years of professional experience, Ahmed specializes in Criminal, Civil, Family, Land, N.I. Act, and Arthorin Ain law.

Whether advocating for individuals, families, or organizations, Ahmed's commitment to integrity and diligence sets him apart as a trusted legal advisor.
